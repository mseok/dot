# Style Pattern

Use the reference blog as a structural model, not as a sentence-level template.

## Core pattern

1. Open with the main claim immediately.
2. Explain why the claim matters.
3. Tell the reader what the post will cover.
4. Move from definitions to mechanism to implications.
5. Reconnect the material to a concrete research problem.

## What to imitate

- thesis-first introduction
- explicit roadmap
- cross-domain translation for mixed audiences
- repeated movement between formal statement and intuition
- disciplined separation between setup, derivation, and application

## What not to imitate blindly

- the exact sentence rhythm
- the exact section count
- the exact level of mathematical detail
- broad historical surveys when the note bundle only supports one narrow point

## Default reader model

Assume the reader is technically mature but not already inside the exact
subtopic. Write for a reader who can follow equations and ML terminology but
still benefits from explicit bridges across fields.

## Good opening questions

- What is the single conceptual bridge this draft is trying to establish?
- Why does this bridge matter for molecular modeling or generative modeling?
- What misunderstanding does this draft remove?

## Section guidance

- `Introduction`: state the punchline and motivation.
- `Roadmap`: tell the reader what each major part will do.
- `Core sections`: each section should advance one necessary step.
- `Broader relevance`: explain why the idea matters for the intended audience
  without turning the draft into a project memo.
- `Open questions`: keep uncertainty visible instead of smoothing it away.

## Tone

- calm
- technical
- explanatory
- non-hyped

Prefer precise transitions such as "the key point is", "the practical
consequence is", or "this matters because" over rhetorical filler.

## Self-contained draft rule

The main narrative should still work if every internal wiki link is removed.
Use links for provenance or optional navigation, not as containers for missing
definitions or skipped reasoning.
